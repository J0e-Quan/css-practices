Report on the effects of smelly pillow aroma on humans

Aim

This experiment investigates the effect of smelly pillow aroma on humans over a long period of time

Timeframe

Tests were conducted 5 minutes, 5 hours and 5 days after the experiment began.

Materials

  • Smelly pillow

Apparatus

  • Air purifier
  • Defibrillator (for medical emergencies)
  • Microphone for recording audio feedback to the aroma
  • Camera for observing physical response of subjects to the aroma

Results

After 5 minutes, subjects reported that the smelly pillow didn't actually smell that bad, remaining comforted by its presence.

After 5 hours, it was reported that the aroma of the smelly pillow had become stronger. Some subjects were seen vomitting, while exclamations of disgust were picked up by the microphone. Fortunately, no physical injuries were recorded at this point.

After 5 days, the aroma had become too strong for researchers to enter the room without wearing hazmat suits. Most subjects had been rendered unconscious, with the ones who remained conscious being taken out of the room for examination. The unconscious subjects seemed to have entered a comatose state with inflammed noses and partial lung failure. The ones who remained conscious were in a state of extreme discomfort, having developed a severe cough and potentially nausea.

Conclusion

This experiment showed how the aroma of a smelly pillow can cause severe health complications if left unchecked by its owner. Contrary to our test results however, the owner still remained mostly unaffected by the aroma. This was supported by the smelly pillow itself, stating "There is no harm in taking a whiff, my aroma would never cause harm to anyone!". (The comatose subjects did not respond to a request for comment.)
